Dear Miss Brown

I can’t express how much you’ve changed my life. You’ve made a significantly better and happier person. There are a lot of things that you’ve helped me with: anxiety, self-love, finding myself, believing in the goodness of humanity, and much more. I just want to say thank you. Since those two words fall short of encompassing my gratitude towards you, I hope this poem can do a better job.

The Cloud

The harsh sun beats down
On delicate petals and leaves.
Suddenly, a cloud comes around,
Providing a much needed reprieve.

The flower is happy to find
That the world is not all heat and pain.
No, the cloud does remind
Of the blessing of shade and rain.

The cloud showers relief and rain.
And as heavenly water falls down,
It drives away fear and pain
From flower fields all around.

The flower bloom in the soft light
The cloud nourishes with duty.
But the bloom cannot rival the white,
Embodiment of peace, elegance and beauty.

The white cloud hangs in the sky
While the flower seems to implore.
Praying to the heavenly cloud as it drifts by,
That it might stay for evermore.

The flower sends its gratitude to the sky
For, the flower, a white rose, ‘tis I.
Hope it reaches so high, piercing the blue,
To meet the ethereal cloud, that is You.

Thank you for everything.


Yours lovingly,
Anand Doshi.
